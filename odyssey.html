Odyssey2.0 is a B2B Management Information System (MIS) designed for the printing industry. It provides a comprehensive suite of tools including accounting, CRM, and marketing functionalities. By integrating with the printer's e-commerce website, Odyssey2.0 eliminates the need for using multiple software solutions to manage various aspects of a print shop. Instead, it serves as the central tool for printers, streamlining processes ranging from estimating and order management to implementing post-sale marketing strategies.

I initially joined the product as the first UX Designer, taking on the role of a generalist. This gave me the opportunity to assume the position of UX Lead, overseeing a small team of designers.

Problem

Printers are constantly facing heavy workloads, yet always striving to improve their sales. Time is their most valuable resource, but they face the challenge of managing multiple software systems to track diverse aspects of their print shop. They encounter the need to repeatedly enter information for a single order, leading to wastage of time, loss of valuable data and and having to master multiple systems for themselves and their team.

‚ÄúTime is everything to me. I can't afford to waste it juggling between different software just to reenter order information. Simplicity is key for me when it comes to printing.‚Äù

I joined the project immediately after it reached the MVP. It had been designed solely by the engineering team as a robust and technical application that required a level of expertise in the printing industry. The product was not doing well, it lacked essential usability heuristics, and, more importantly, the organization failed to understand the true value that our users found in the product. It was struggling on gaining subscribers and was losing the few it had at an alarming rate.

Process

By implementing continuous research, we were able to consistently understand our users' experiences. This enabled us to apply a Design Thinking methodology within an agile environment.

Research

To gain a contextual understanding of our users' needs, behaviors, and problems, especially considering the challenges posed by a remote research strategy, we discovered that a diary study proved to be the ideal method for capturing the essence of their experiences. We frequently use this method to ensure we stay aware to our users' current experience. It allows us to consistently stay in touch with their needs and make informed decisions.

Diary collection.

I invite printers with different printshop roles to document their workday: interactions, actions, goals, etc. They can document this through written entries, photos, videos, or voice recordings; always including their thoughts and mental process along with it. Participants capture a vivid picture of their journey over a defined period.

User interviews.

Once they have their diary, I conduct in-depth interviews with participants. This allows me to dive deeper into their thoughts, motivations, and challenges.

Data analysis.

With both diary entries and interview transcripts, I embark on a rigorous data analysis process. I carefully review and analyze the collected data, identifying patterns, themes, and key insights.

Ideating

The absence of existing solutions intensified the need for innovation and challenging problem-solving techniques. With an open mind, I immersed myself in the insights gained through user research. However, by embracing a design thinking process, collaborating with cross-functional teams, and fearlessly iterating on ideas, I was able to achieve user-centric solutions that surpassed industry standards.

Brainstorming

Given the unique nature of the product and the specific needs of our users, ideation posed a significant challenge. To tackle this, we heavily relied on brainstorming techniques. At the heart of this process was comprehending the goals, actions, and tasks performed by our users on a daily basis, and translating this information into our digital product.

The "how might we" technique is particularly valuable for tackling challenging problems because it encourages an outcome-oriented mindset, sparking creativity and empowering us to explore innovative approaches.

How might we...

Empower interdepartmental communication to align the goals of the print shop.

How might we...

Help new members of the staff to understand the printing world through an onboarding process.

How might we...

Eliminate the need to re-enter information, allowing us to rely solely on the customer-submitted order form.

How might we...

Automate and visually illustrate the paper transformation process in a typical printing job.
